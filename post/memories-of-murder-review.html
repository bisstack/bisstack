Momories of Murder - Directed by Bong Joon-ho

Berserk March 29 · 9 min
Share

Memories of murder (2003) is a captivating crime thriller directed by Bong Joon-ho

The story is inspired by South Korea's first serial murders happened between 1986 and 1991.

I appeared the movie with a closure of just a crime thriller movie that tells the storey of brilliant-but-flawed detective,series of clues,a suspect that seems too obvious,a hidden clue like the davinci-code which the detective note at the end. This is not that movie What Bong Joon-ho gives us is sometging closer to slow suffocation.The longer it goes on, the more you feel like you're drowning in details that never connect.You are seeing the two detectives Park Doo-man(Song Kang-ho) and Seo Tae-yoon(Kim Sang-kyung) try everything interrogation,Brutality,Science, Desparation.None of it works

Every frame feels like it s weifhed down.There s always rain.The earth is wet.The sky is gray.There s mud on boots,blood on hands and sweat on foreheads.It s not "cinematic".It’s just feels real,messy,cold like a memory you wish you didn't have.

Bong doesn't rush anything.He gives you time to sit in the silence to notice when characters stop believing in themselves.And when they do,it hits hard.

Song Kang-ho is my favourate character.The character writing is extraordinary.He started almost like a joke-a small town cop who thinks he can solve murders with gut instinct.You want to laugh.Then you want to shake him.Then,suddenly,you are him.You feel that hunger for certainty,that panic when it all slips away.

The ending gave me nothing and that is everything.There's no big reveal just a man looking into tha camera (it is the idea of director Bong Joon-ho’s that the detective look at the audience as he belived that the real killer will watch the movie)
